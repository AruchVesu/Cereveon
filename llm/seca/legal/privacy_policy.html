Privacy Policy

Effective 17 July 2026 · applies to the Cereveon apps for Android and iOS and the Cereveon service at cereveon.com

1. Who we are

Cereveon is a chess-coaching service operated by an independent individual developer, who is the data controller for the personal data described in this policy. You can reach us about privacy at privacy@cereveon.com. We have not appointed a Data Protection Officer, as the service is small-scale and processes no special-category data.

The short version

  • You need an account (email + password, or a Lichess sign-in). We never see your Lichess password, and we don't ask for your name, phone number, address, or location.
  • Your games, puzzles, and coach conversations are stored in your account so the coach can track your progress. Chess analysis runs on our own servers with a local engine — no analysis service sees your games.
  • Coach replies are written by an AI language model (DeepSeek). To generate a reply we send the current board position, engine context, your recent coach messages, and a rough skill sketch — never your email, name, account ID, or IP address.
  • The apps contain no advertising, analytics, or tracking SDKs and request a single permission (internet access). We do not sell or share your data for advertising.
  • Payments are handled by Google Play. We never see your card details.
  • You can download a copy of your data or delete your account at any time — in the app, or on the web at cereveon.com/delete-account.

Games you play or import (moves, results, board positions), computed accuracy and mistake categories, post-game AI reviews, puzzle and drill attempts, study plans, XP and level, and your opening repertoire. The app also keeps a small in-app notification feed about your own account (e.g. "your imported games were analysed"); notifications are generated by our server from your existing data, never from third-party sources, and are deleted with your account.

Skill profile

To coach you, we continuously derive a skill profile from your play: a rating estimate, a confidence value, a per-phase weakness/strength vector, a numeric player embedding, and per-game analytics records. This profile is used only to personalise coaching content and difficulty (for example, so the coach knows endgames are your weak spot) — see §5 on automated decisions.

Coach conversations

Your messages to the coach and the coach's replies are stored in your account, together with the board position each message referred to, so your conversation survives reinstalls and device changes.

Feedback

If you send feedback, we store your message text (up to 2,000 characters) and app version. Feedback is read by the operator only; it is never fed into the AI, never used for coaching, and its content is kept out of server logs.

Subscription

Your plan tier (free / Pro). For Pro purchases, Google Play sends us an opaque purchase token and product ID, which we verify with Google. We never receive your payment details — Google processes the payment.

Technical data

Server request logs (timestamp, endpoint, status, latency, request ID, and connection IP address) for security, rate limiting, and abuse prevention. These logs are kept only within a rolling, size-capped window on the server and are not retained long-term. We build no advertising or cross-service profiles from this data.

On your device

The app stores your login token in encrypted storage (AES-256-GCM via the platform keystore) and caches your current game locally in app-private storage. App data is excluded from cloud backups and device-to-device transfer. The apps use no cookies and no advertising identifiers.

What we do not collect

No real name, no phone number, no postal address, no location, no contacts, no photos, no camera or microphone access, no advertising ID, and no third-party analytics or crash-reporting SDKs on either platform. The Android app declares exactly one permission: internet access.

3. Where your data lives

Cereveon runs on a server rented from Hetzner (a German hosting provider), located in the European Union, including the database that holds all account data. Chess-engine analysis runs locally on that server. A short-lived engine cache (Redis, on the same server) is keyed by board position only and contains no personal data.

5. Why we process your data, and AI transparency

We process account, play, training and coaching data to provide the service you asked for (the legal basis is performance of our contract with you, GDPR Art. 6(1)(b)). We process technical/log data and handle feedback on the basis of our legitimate interest in keeping the service secure, available, and improving (Art. 6(1)(f)), and we keep minimal billing records to meet legal obligations (Art. 6(1)(c)). We rely on consent for nothing today; if we ever add marketing or optional analytics, we will ask first. An email or a Lichess identity is required to create an account.

The Cereveon coach is an AI system: replies are generated by a large language model and are clearly presented as coming from the app's coach. Chess facts come from a local engine, and every AI reply passes validation filters — including one that blocks personal-data patterns — before you see it; if the AI is unavailable or fails validation, a deterministic non-AI reply is served instead. You can report any coach message you find offensive or harmful from within the app (the flag icon on a coach message), and we review those reports. We make no decisions about you with legal or similarly significant effects based solely on automated processing (Art. 22 GDPR): the skill profile only tunes coaching, and free-tier limits are fixed quotas applied to everyone equally.

6. International transfers

Our servers are in the European Union. Two flows send data outside the EEA:

  • DeepSeek (People's Republic of China). Coach replies are generated by DeepSeek's API, which is operated from China, so generating a reply transfers the data listed in §4 outside the EEA. That data is deliberately minimised: it contains no direct identifiers (no email, name, account ID, token, or IP), and the board positions and coaching text are not linkable to you by DeepSeek. We keep this transfer to the minimum necessary to provide AI coaching.
  • Google (purchase verification and the feedback digest email) may process data outside the EU under its own certified safeguards (EU–US Data Privacy Framework / Standard Contractual Clauses).

Lichess is operated from within the EU (France), so linking and imports stay within the EEA.

8. Your rights

Under the GDPR you can ask us to access your data and receive a copy in a portable, machine-readable format, rectify it, erase it, restrict or object to processing based on legitimate interest, and not be subject to solely automated decisions with legal effect (we make none).

  • Download your data yourself in the app: Settings › Account › Download my data.
  • Delete your account and all of its data yourself — in the app (Settings › Account › Delete account) or, if you've uninstalled it, on the web at cereveon.com/delete-account. Either way our server erases everything tied to your account (games, chat history, skill profile, feedback, linked-account records) in one transaction, immediately and irreversibly.
  • For anything else, email privacy@cereveon.com from your account email (or with your Lichess username for Lichess sign-ins) and we will respond within one month.

You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.

9. Security

  • All traffic is TLS-encrypted (HTTPS-only with HSTS); both apps additionally pin our server's certificate keys, so they refuse to talk to an impostor server even if a device's trust store is compromised.
  • Passwords are stored as salted PBKDF2-SHA256 (600,000 iterations). Login tokens are signed, expire after 24 hours, rotate on every request, and are revocable server-side; changing your password signs out all sessions.
  • On-device: tokens are held in hardware-backed encrypted storage, and app data is excluded from cloud backups.
  • AI boundary: model output is validated before display (including a filter for personal-data patterns), and user input is sanitised against prompt injection.
  • Server: analysis is local (no third-party analysis service), requests are rate-limited, services are containerised, and structured logs exclude chat content, emails, and passwords.
  • If a breach is likely to risk your rights, we will notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours (Art. 33) and affected users where required (Art. 34).

10. Children

The service is not directed at anyone under 16, and we do not knowingly collect data from children under 16. If you believe a child has created an account, contact us and we will delete it.
