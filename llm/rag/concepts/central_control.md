# Central Control

## What it is
Control over the central squares (e4, d4, e5, d5).

## Why it matters
Central control allows pieces to move freely and limits the opponent’s options.

## Typical signs
- Pawns or pieces occupying central squares
- Ability to push or challenge central pawns
- Opponent pieces restricted

## Common mistakes
- Giving up the center without compensation
- Attacking the flanks before central stability

## How Stockfish sees it
Stockfish values central control as it increases mobility and tactical potential.

## Practical advice
Fight for the center early, either by occupation or pressure.
